Dark energy is a hypothetical form of energy that exerts a negative, repulsive pressure, behaving like the opposite of gravity. It is distributed relatively uniformly throughout space and interacts with normal matter only through gravity. It is currently estimated to account for approximately 68% to 70% of the total energy density of the observable universe.

        1^11

While dark matter acts as a cosmic "glue" that holds galaxies together via attractive gravity, dark energy acts as a cosmic "pusher," driving the universe’s expansion to accelerate over time.
